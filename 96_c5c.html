TUNA AND COURGETTE FRITTERS

Such an awesome and tasty snack – and there’s nearly always a spare tin of tuna knocking about in the cupboard. You can batch-cook and freeze these too, if you like.

MAKES 12–14 SMALL (or 4 big)

MAKE AHEAD

GOOD TO FREEZE

INGREDIENTS

1 x 160g tin of tuna, drained

1 courgette, grated

80g self-raising flour

1 egg

1 tbsp coconut oil

light soy sauce, to serve

METHOD

Flake the tuna out of the tin into a bowl, then add the courgette, flour and egg. Mix together to make a batter. If need be, add a dribble of water to loosen the consistency until it resembles thick double cream.

Melt a little of the oil in a frying pan over a medium heat. Spoon in large mounds of the batter, leaving some space between each one, as the batter will spread and work itself into a little puddle. Aim for fritters about 7–8cm in diameter if you want to make about a dozen – but feel free to just make four big ones instead.

Cook the fritters for about 2–3 minutes on each side before lifting them out of the pan and draining on paper towels.

Serve the fritters with a small bowl of soy sauce for dipping.
